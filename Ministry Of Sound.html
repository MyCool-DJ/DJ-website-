MINISTRY OF SOUND

Started as an underground movement.

Ministry Of Sound

Offical Website

I will never forget hearing my first MOS CD; when I heard the end of one song mix into the beginning of another song, a penny dropped for me. This is the way. I was hooked. I had been trying to learn the piano after trying as a child; I gave up and then was giving up again as a teen. But when I heard this music, it dawned on me what I wanted to learn; I wanted to learn to make these computer-generated dance hits. I asked my piano teacher, how can I make music like this? She said first the piano is an accompaniment, unlike the classical music I had been learning, and she said the beat, the keyboards, and the lyrics are, in this case, the melody. I said, but how do I create my music at this level? She said there is only one way, to play other people's music and figure out what makes it so popular. Fun fact here is no genre as pop. It's just whatever is popular at the time. This Electronic dance music became my obsession, so I wanted to be a DJ and learn what made a dance beat so good. Ministry of Sound was the record label I listened to as a guide. Ministry of sound is actually marketing for a nightclub in the UK called Ministry of Sound; they were an underground movement for people to come together and enjoy music. This was an area of pubs that closed at 6 pm in the early '80s. This was a movement that was hated by the police and local councils. They thought these bars would destroy young people. Nightclubs were actually a safe haven and, in the end, mainly were shut down and, by gentrification, closed. Now it's mostly flat parties and Spotify. FYI Spotify doesn't mix music to say this is a dying art is an understatement it is a dead art. COVID was the final nail in the coffin for many clubs. The nightclub industry is now mainly upmarket bistros and cocktail lounges some have dancefloors; these are not real nightclubs however and the raves that lasted until sunrise. All of today's remaining bars have to now close at 3 am here in Dunedin, and the student bars are nearly all gone too.
