MYCOOL-DJ

Ex-Touring Club DJ.

MyCool-DJ (stage name)

MixCloud

My DJ career spanned over a decade close to 15years, as I was DJing (mixing other people's music) for a living at the top nightclub hotspots in the Lower South Island New Zealand. I did many private functions too such as school balls, 21sts, and weddings. Mostly I played in the clubs, this was a special skill that took a lot of years to master, to learn something often doesn't take long (surface learning) but to master something can take a lifetime or at least 10,000 hours or 10years. This is the one thing I was able to master before my industry died. I saw the student bars close first and then saw the private functions fade away to using Spotify. I was a sales Rep at the time for a Music company called Orange Door, originally called Nightlife. Nightlife then teamed up with Spotify and all other companies folded including iTunes. This is due to streaming technologies. I loved my career, to entertain, it's my purpose (my Why). This is what got me into technology, my interest in wanting to learn I.T (my How) is how to use technology primarily for entertainment purposes. This website will cover my favorite DJs/Producers who influenced my DJing style, my long-term goal was to be producing by now but it's too hard to make a living so I gave up. Fun Fact: Wanting to learn to code all steamed from a DJ invention I came up with and when I tried to patent it and then sell it I was mocked for not being able to code. My idea was then stolen.

Due to the internet, many artists can now launch from just the internet, but I was in this transition of the major record labels that published (Mastered and mixed) then distributed CDs VS the rise of the internet which was not like it is today with all the tools to launch yourself. This is a double-edged sword as streaming services such as Spotify works out to be about $3.80USD per 1000 streams and in NZD that's about 5 dollars or a coffee. Therefore, I am now learning to Code, instead. When I was a DJ things like promotions and mixes were handled by other agents, I had my mixes on iTunes from being on a local radio station OAR, and a profile on a website called Snapstar (they chose if you were worthy). DJ's peaked as morden-day rockstars in the early 2000s' and today's entrepreneurs are today's rockstars IMO which is my next plan. Being on a stage and having to perform is what I know best, and the startup scene offers me this. To me a startup pitch [to raise captial], is all about putting on a good show and something people will pay me to do for them (monetization). I am still available for guest appearances VIA PME Entertainment. Even though I am no longer DJing my career will always be in the entertainment industry and finding new ways to entertain my audience.
